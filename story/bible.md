# THE ABOVE — NARRATIVE BIBLE (COMPLETE, 3 ACTS)

> Deliverable M-STORY. All facts interlock with `story/secret-bible.md` (internal truth,
> never published). Character detail lives in `story/characters.md`; clip storyboards in
> `story/clip-anchors.md`. Dialogue below is reference prose — final lines are authored
> as EN+TR key pairs in `game/locale/`.

**Tone laws (binding):** warmth first, then wrongness. 90% quiet and ordinary. One wrong
note per town day. Scale stated in instruments, never shown. No fail states — fear is the
price of answers the player chooses to seek. The signal is a character. The entity never
performs; everything it does would look the same if no one were watching.

**The story-logic chain (Act 1 spine):** delayed discovery → single feed horn → the
verification gauntlet → the second dish → the transgression → the 11-minute keystone →
probes/the triad → "it knows which dish is yours."

# COLD OPEN (20 seconds, wordless)

1. Black. Ventilation hum fades in — a breathing building.
2. A ship corridor at 640×360: steel blue-green, one amber status lamp blinking on a
   slow cycle. **Six suit hooks on the wall: five suits, one empty.**
3. Nothing moves except the lamp. Hold 8 full seconds — longer than comfort.
4. On the sixth blink, the lamp's color is signal-magenta for exactly one frame.
5. Hard cut — no fade — to Taşlıca at golden hour, birdsong, wind in poplars.
   Title card: **THE ABOVE.**

The first clip exists before minute one. Nothing here is explained for the whole game;
the post-credit replay (below) changes one detail.

# ACT 1 — "TAŞLICA" (Days 1–7)

## The daily loop (base form — learned Day 1, deviated every day after)

- **WAKE:** Deniz's stone house. Kettle on. Yıldız the cat demands tribute. Radio
  murmurs provincial news. The player learns the house.
- **DESCEND:** the walk into town — Fadime's bakery (end-piece of bread saved for him),
  Musa's bakkal, the square, Kadir's tea house (weak tea, one sugar, never finishes the
  second glass).
- **ASCEND:** İsmet's dolmuş up the switchbacks to Sarptepe — the town shrinking below,
  the dish **Kulak** growing above. The game's upward/outward movement grammar starts
  here and never reverses.
- **NIGHT SHIFT:** the observatory — Metin at the racks, Ebru buried in printouts,
  Director Tezcan's office dark or ominously lit. Work is done through terminals,
  chart printers, and the spectrogram waterfall.
- **RETURN & SLEEP:** the dolmuş down under stars, one ritual glance up, bed. Save.

Each day below lists: scenes beat-by-beat, the daily-loop variation, and the wrong note
(the collectible anomaly — findable, never pointed at; the game never confirms them).

## DAY 1 — The Delayed Discovery *(story beat 1; clip anchor ①)*

**D1-S1 · Wake (tutorial-as-life).** Kettle, cat, radio: a story about road repairs. The
player learns movement and the INTERACT verb by doing chores. On the wall: a framed
photo of İbrahim Aydın in front of Kulak, 1980s. Examining it: *"My father kept this
dish clean for thirty years. He never once looked through it."*

**D1-S2 · The town.** Full loop, warm register. Fadime's end-piece. Şükrü mid-backgammon
("Sit. Lose gracefully."). Elif ambushes him with a space question (answer via choice —
her notebook records whatever the player says; it returns in Act 3 and Ending B). Kadir's
tea. **Wrong note #1:** at the tea house there is one extra chair at the wall table.
Kadir, if asked: "Not mine. Thought it was yours." No one moves it. It is there all week.

**D1-S3 · Ascent & naming ritual.** Dolmuş with İsmet (holds his seat: "Night-shift men
sit up front"). At the observatory door, the logbook terminal prompts **OBSERVER:** — the
player types the observer name (Undertale-style ritual; meta-persistent). Metin hums —
he only hums when the data is good.

**D1-S4 · Ordinary work (waterfall tutorial).** A calibration task on the spectrogram
waterfall: drag frequency, align a known pulsar, feel the tool. Diegetic, quiet,
satisfying. Verbs at the console: LISTEN / RECORD / LOG.

**D1-S5 · The archived printouts (the discovery).** Housekeeping: Tezcan wants last
week's batch archive shelved. In the stack — days old, printed while everyone slept — a
narrowband trace at 1421.6 MHz, drifting −0.11 Hz/s, sitting in the batch like a hair in
a photograph. Beat rhythm: boredom → double-take → the printer's date stamp → the line
*"It arrived Tuesday. It's Friday. We heard it days after it spoke."* The camera does not
zoom; Deniz's idle animation just stops. Player verbs: RE-PRINT / COMPARE / SAY NOTHING.
Whatever is chosen, he pockets the printout. (Secret-bible note: the file re-keyed at
print time — the reading of the town began days ago. Hence the chair.)

**D1-S6 · Return.** The ritual glance up. The stars are fine. Sleep. Save slot shows the
typed observer name for the first time.

**Loop variation:** none — Day 1 IS the loop. Its purity is load-bearing.

## DAY 2 — The Single Feed Horn *(story beat 2)*

**D2-S1 · Wake, deviation one.** The radio plays the same road-repair story as
yesterday. (Foreshadows wrong note #3; deniable — regional radio repeats things.)

**D2-S2 · Town, compressed.** The loop plays but Deniz walks faster; the town notices.
Fadime: "You didn't look at the bread, Hoca." First cost of the signal: attention.

**D2-S3 · The sanity check fails.** Observatory, daylight — he came early. With Ebru: a
fixed sky source must appear in all three beams as the sky drifts through, in both
polarizations. T1 lives **only in beam 2. Always. Even when the pointing changes.**
Ebru, delighted, then uneasy: "That's not a source, Hocam. That's a wire fault with
taste." Waterfall session: the player pans beams 1→2→3; the trace exists only under
beam 2 — the first *felt* impossibility, delivered by the player's own hands.

**D2-S4 · Colleagues dismiss (the social engine starts).** Tezcan, kind and tired:
equipment fault; the dish is old; grant renewal is next month; "Don't hang a career on a
broken feed horn. I watched better men do it." (One frame of the 1986 file drawer
visible behind him — set dressing only.) Metin stops humming when the printout comes
out. He says nothing. (Metin, 58, remembers Hale; the game never says so — he simply
never hums again near T1 material.)

**D2-S5 · Night: alone with it.** First live LISTEN of T1. The signal's broadcast
schedule is learned here: it comes in 11-minute cells (never stated; the counting
players do it themselves — wrong note #4 later rhymes). New verb appears, grayed:
**ANSWER** — unavailable, but visibly *there*. Dread by interface.

**Wrong note #2:** Veli the shepherd, met on the switchback road: "Eastern star moved
last night. A fingerwidth. Then it felt me looking and stopped." Through the finder
scope, if the player checks: a point of light that is not in the catalog, not moving.

**Loop variation:** he skips the tea house entirely; Kadir sends Zeliha's lokum up with
İsmet — the town's warmth follows him uphill.

## DAY 3 — The Verification Gauntlet & the Microwave *(story beat 3; clip anchor ②)*

**D3-S1 · Wake.** Yıldız refuses to enter the kitchen while the radio is on. He notes
it aloud, amused. (Cat = carrier detector; pays off Day 5.)

**D3-S2 · The checklist (montage of rigor).** Deniz and Ebru run the real kill-list, and
the game says so on a literal clipboard the player checks off:
1. **Drift rate:** consistent with a body in solar orbit, not Earth's surface. PASS.
2. **On/off-source nodding:** point away — gone; point back — there, mid-syllable, as if
   it hadn't noticed. PASS (and quietly horrible).
3. **RFI database:** three matches found against the archive batch — despair beat, the
   candidacy nearly dies…

**D3-S3 · The peryton (comedy beat, played 100% straight).** The three RFI matches
correlate with lunch hours. The culprit: the break-room microwave — Ebru's instant soup;
opening the door before the timer ends spits a broadband chirp into the band (real
Parkes peryton canon). Scene beats: Metin unplugs the microwave with the gravity of a
bomb disposal; Ebru holds her soup cup like a suspect; test detection; the chirps are
GONE from the new data — **and T1 is still there, clean, patient, in beam 2.** Deniz's
line, deadpan, to the microwave: "You're free to go." The comedy purges every mundane
explanation and hardens the anomaly; the audience laughed, and the thing survived their
laughter.

**D3-S4 · The night decision.** T1 has now survived every test that killed every
previous candidate in history. Deniz drafts the confirmation request e-mail to a distant
observatory (Millbrook Radio Observatory, West Virginia). Verb: SEND / SLEEP ON IT.
(Both send it — SLEEP ON IT sends at 04:12 with a typo. Choices are emotional.)

**Wrong note #3:** Cemal's radio in the butcher shop replays *yesterday's* broadcast —
same news, same stumble, same lottery numbers. Cemal, arguing football, hasn't noticed.
The lottery numbers are checkable against Musa's posted ticket stub.

**Loop variation:** the ascent happens at noon (checklist ran all day); the town seen at
an unfamiliar hour, slightly wrong in the way familiar places are at wrong hours —
training the player's anomaly-eye on innocent material.

## DAY 4 — The Second Dish *(story beat 4; clip anchor ③)*

**D4-S1 · Wake, deviation.** He wakes at 14:00 — the loop shown mid-collapse. Kettle
cold. Yıldız asleep on the printout.

**D4-S2 · The wait.** Millbrook's reply: they'll observe when the source rises for them
— a six-hour wait. The game makes the player *live* a compressed slice of it in the tea
house: backgammon with Şükrü (playable, one roll), Elif's second question, Kadir's tea.
The warmest scene in the act, wired to a timer everyone feels. **Wrong note #4:** the
streetlamp outside Fadime's bakery flickers in 11-beat groups. Count them or don't.

**D4-S3 · The call.** Night, observatory, the landline (a 1980s phone — Sarptepe never
threw it out). Phone rings twice before Deniz can cross the room. Dr. Lena Petrides,
Millbrook, four seconds of transatlantic static, then: **"Dr. Aydın. We hear it too."**
Hold on Deniz's face; Metin in the doorway; Ebru sits down on the floor. Beat two of the
call, quieter, the hook under the hook: "One thing. It's only in one beam here as well.
*Our* beam two. Doctor — whose instrument does that?"

**D4-S4 · World event.** The IAA post-detection protocol engages. First diegetic news
ticker crawls across the tea house TV: *"…confirmed by second site … International
Academy of Astronautics protocol invoked … Rio Scale assessed at 4…"* The town watches
Deniz watch the TV. Tarık the muhtar, gravely: "Is it above us, Hoca?" — "Everything is
above us, Tarık Bey." First laugh at scale; last easy laugh.

**Loop variation:** the descent happens at dawn *after* the call — the loop inverted;
the town asleep; he has champagne with no one, a tea with Kadir who opens early for him.
Kadir asks nothing. Pours.

## DAY 5 — The Transgression *(story beat 5)*

**D5-S1 · Wake to noise.** The radio is all T1. A voice mispronounces "Taşlıca." Two
reporters in the square photograph the dish from Musa's roof. The town closes politely
around Deniz like a shell — Havva the postwoman misdirects a camera crew with great
skill and no shame.

**D5-S2 · Ada arrives.** Dr. Ada Reyes, IAA SETI liaison, sent when Rio hit 4 — violet
scarf, cassette recorder, zero small talk: "Play me everything. Don't summarize. People
summarize the wrong parts." She taxonomizes T1's calls like birdsong on the tea house's
back table (dawn song, contact call, mimic phrase, counting song) — the player's second
waterfall session, now with a companion who *names* things. Attachment begins in Act 1
on purpose: she is crew later.

**D5-S3 · The rule and the breach.** IAA consultation rule on replies, stated on-screen
in a document the player can actually read. Then the ticker: **a state deep-space
facility has transmitted a high-power reply without consultation.** Rio jumps to 7. Ada,
cold with anger, delivers the protocol's obituary: "Forty years of agreeing what we'd do.
Undone before dinner."

**D5-S4 · The ANSWER verb (the player's transgression).** Night. Everyone gone — Ada to
her pension, Metin home, Ebru asleep in the rack room. The console. The schedule cell
opens. The verb **ANSWER** is no longer gray.
- The scene makes it entirely avoidable: LISTEN and LOG are right there. Nothing
  prompts. Nothing rewards. (Ending C gate: refusing every voluntary ANSWER, all game.)
- Canonical-default: he answers — one clean carrier pulse, his dish's voice, three
  seconds. The room does not change. The music does not change. That's the horror:
  transgression feels like nothing.
**Wrong note #5:** walking home: the evening ezan's mountain echo returns *early* — the
echo arrives before it should, by half a breath. İsmet, driving: "Mountain's keen
tonight." **Wrong note #6:** at home, Yıldız stares fixed at a ceiling corner; the paint
there has faded in the *shape of the dish*. It has clearly been fading for years.

**Loop variation:** the town loop is invaded — press, strangers' cars, a drone İsmet
reports to no one in particular. The player feels the town's privacy as a thing being
lost, which prices the send-off scene two days later.

## DAY 6 — The Eleven Minutes *(story beat 6, THE KEYSTONE; clip anchor ④; beat 7 begins)*

**D6-S1 · 02:13.** Deniz, alone on shift, live waterfall. At 02:13 the state facility's
reply (rebroadcast on the wire) goes out. At **02:24** — eleven minutes later — the
waterfall lights up. T1 has changed: the long call. A structured, dense, *addressed*
transmission, eleven minutes long, in beam 2, on his dish. He records all of it with
hands that misclick twice (sprite-level acting: two failed clicks are animated).
Petrides, on the landline minutes later: "Millbrook has it too. But Deniz — here it's
noise. Structured noise. It's only *language* on yours."

**D6-S2 · THE WHITEBOARD, BEAT ONE — relief.** 04:00, break room, the whiteboard, Deniz
and Ebru (Metin in the doorway; he will not come in). The scene gets the game's slowest
pacing and its most disproportionate polish (risk register #4). The arithmetic is done
ON SCREEN, marker squeaking, player advancing each stroke:
- Reply delay: 11 minutes. Light goes both ways. **d ≤ c·t/2 = 5.5 light-minutes.**
- Ebru writes it in km, laughs at herself, circles it: **≈ 99 million km.**
- Deniz, quietly: *"That's inside the orbit of Mars."* Beat. *"It's not light-years
  away. It's HERE. Inside the system."*
- The relief is real and the scene lets it breathe: they eat Zeliha's lokum at 4 a.m.
  Ebru does a small terrible dance. A crewed mission is suddenly *physics*, not fiction.

**D6-S3 · Triangulation (the day between the beats).** The global VLBI network fixes the
carrier's origin. Montage in instruments: correlator plots, baseline solutions, a fax
(Sarptepe keeps a fax) creeping out line by line. The solution: a dark object, inbound,
hyperbolic, **29.8 AU out.** Ticker: object designated; press names it **Kuyu** — "the
Well" — for its albedo: "a hole in the sky, falling toward us."

**D6-S4 · THE WHITEBOARD, BEAT TWO — dread.** Same board, 11 hours later, title card:
*"THE SAME WHITEBOARD."* Both numbers now on it, both circled: **5.5 light-minutes**
and **29.8 AU = 4 h 08 m.** The scene is the two circles and three people not talking.
- Ebru does the round-trip: 8 h 16 m. Against: 11 minutes.
- Deniz computes the departure time and writes it like a confession: *the reply left the
  Object 3 hours 57 minutes before the question was transmitted.*
- The line (clipped): **"Either the physics is wrong, or it answered before it was
  asked."**
- No sting. No music. Metin, from the doorway, the only time he speaks near T1
  material: "Erase one. Whichever lets you sleep." He leaves. They don't erase either.

**D6-S5 · The defensible half.** Officials publish distance and reachability; the
causality contradiction is omitted from every release (the scientists carry it
privately — a burden the player now shares). Ticker: **"Rio raised to 8."** Tea house TV;
the town watching; Şükrü to Deniz, not unkindly: "They're saying it's close. Close like
the mountain, or close like winter?"

**D6-S6 · The triad begins (beat 7a).** News: an outbound Kuiper-mission probe,
retasked for early flyby, has **gone silent** — telemetry ends mid-frame; engineers
describe the electronics as "blank, not broken." (ARG: the final frame, decodable, ends
in repeated `EOF`.) **Wrong note #7:** Hüsnü's barber mirror — the reflected calendar
shows *tomorrow's* date. Hüsnü has stopped looking at the mirror. He shaves customers
facing the window now, and doesn't say why. **Wrong note #8:** rain on Değirmen Street
only, from a clear sky; the puddles do not ripple. Elif stands in it with her notebook.

**Loop variation:** the morning walk is gone — Deniz goes straight up and now sleeps at
the observatory. He descends exactly once, at dusk, for the tea house (D6-S5); otherwise
the town appears only as lights out the window. The game's composition starts pulling
him skyward.

## DAY 7 — Your Dish, the Decision, the Send-Off, the Launch *(story beat 8; clip anchors ⑤ ⑥)*

**D7-S1 · The blind test ("it knows which dish is yours").** The mission question is
already in the air (rival power assembling a launch; one intercept window at Kuyu's
14-AU perihelion — 'Oumuamua/Lyra logic on a slide). But the scene that decides
everything is small: five stations, randomized double-blind schedule (Metin holds the
envelope and doesn't know either), all listening to the same sky.
- When Millbrook listens: structured noise. When Hartebeesthoek listens: noise. When
  **Kulak** listens: **language.** Every pass. The correlation will not break.
- They test cruelly: they let Deniz operate Millbrook's feed remotely — noise. They put
  Ebru at Kulak's console — language. **It is not him at a desk. It is his dish.** (The
  full truth — the file's address — stays secret-bible; the surface is airtight.)
- Ada, into her cassette recorder, flat: *"Correlation persists. Test 31 of 31."* Click.
  Then, to the room, the thesis line: **"Every protocol we have assumed the message
  would be old, distant, and indifferent. This one is none of those."**
- Deniz, looking at Kulak through the window; the line that gets clipped:
  **"It knows which dish is yours."**

**D7-S2 · The triad completes (beat 7b–c).** The mission case, argued in one room, on
one whiteboard (the same whiteboard — it is a character now):
(a) probes fail — one silent already, its sister will follow during training (ticker,
weeks later); (b) latency doctrine — a *reactive* source cannot be handled at hours of
round-trip; you need judgment on site (NASA Mars-ops precedent quoted on a slide);
(c) the rival launches regardless, and the window is single. Haste is chosen with eyes
open — the cut corners are on-screen decisions by named officials, so the later deaths
need no monster.

**D7-S3 · The asking.** They need the receiver aboard. Ada is the one who says it,
because she's the one who hates it: "The instrument is the man, Deniz. I've read my own
taxonomy. I know what I'm asking." The choice UI offers two options: **SAY YES / SAY
NOTHING.** Both proceed — silence is taken as consent by the room, by the state, by the
world. A single quiet UI beat: only the Archive, per its own law, would have heard the
silence as real. (Never explained; the lattice hums.)

**D7-S4 · Weeks pass (montage).** Training news under town scenes: suit fittings on TV,
Ebru named interim lead at Sarptepe, second probe lost (ticker: "blank, not broken" —
the phrase entering the language). **Wrong note #9:** one evening, every dog in Taşlıca
faces the observatory at the same moment. Silent. Ten seconds. Then done, forever.

**D7-S5 · THE SEND-OFF (emotional peak — full scene list in characters.md).** His last
morning. The whole loop, played one final time, but the town has assembled inside it:
Fadime's end-piece wrapped for a journey; Musa "forgetting" to charge him; Şükrü
conceding a backgammon game unasked ("Consider it your birthday"); Elif giving him a
page torn from her notebook (his own Day-1 answer, in her handwriting); Cemal, Havva,
Nurten, Hüsnü, Osman, Veli, Tarık — each one line, each anchored to the small thing they
remember about him. İsmet drives the dolmuş with the whole town's letters in a shoebox.
- Last: **Saniye Teyze, in her doorway.** No crowd, no phones — the one goodbye no
  camera sees. She presses her house key, wrapped in a handkerchief, into his hand:
  **"Don't leave the flowers without water. You'll be back before they notice."** (The
  gift the finale is built on; because no device recorded it, no copy contains it.)
- Deniz looks up the valley at Kulak once. Metin, at the observatory, tips the dish two
  degrees — the closest thing to a wave a 32-meter instrument can do.

**D7-S6 · THE LAUNCH.** Smash cut: gantry, steam, checklists in three languages.
Intercut: the tea house TV, the whole town crammed in, Zeliha's hand on Kadir's
shoulder, the extra chair empty in the back of frame. Inside the capsule: five
strangers' breathing and one astronomer's. Ada across the aisle — one known face. Engine
roar swallows everything except, under it, planted in the mix: the signal's interval
motif. The player realizes the music knew. Cut to black on max thrust.

# ACT 2 — "THE CROSSING" (~4.4 years, nine sleep cycles)

## Structure — the hibernation watch as attrition engine

Mission **ORPHEUS**: six crew, nine hibernation cycles (C1–C9, ~6 months each), each
cycle centered on a **20-day two-person watch**; all others sleep. The lattice of the
act: **Deniz stands watches C1, C3, C5, C7, C9 — each with a different partner — and
every taking happens while he sleeps.** He is always the last to have really talked with
them. He keeps waking to a quieter ship.

| Cycle | Deniz | Watch partner | Event while he sleeps |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | awake | **Ada** (attachment arc) | — |
| C2 | asleep | Voss + Theo | **Ada taken** |
| C3 | awake | **Theo** | discovery of Ada at wake-up |
| C4 | asleep | Voss + Yusuf | **Theo taken** |
| C5 | awake | **Yusuf** | discovery of Theo at wake-up |
| C6 | asleep | Voss + Mara | **Yusuf taken** |
| C7 | awake | **Voss** | discovery of Yusuf at wake-up |
| C8 | asleep | Mara (solo watch — protocol broken by necessity) | **Voss taken** |
| C9 | awake | **Mara** → arrival | discovery of Voss; then the finale |

Design invariants: disappearances are **discovered at wake-up, aftermath-first, never
shown**; each ships **2–3 mutually incompatible evidence pieces + exactly one anomalous
detail**; the cause is refused forever; every ship log gap is **exactly 11 minutes**.
(Per-run variation may reorder Ada/Theo/Yusuf/Voss; Mara-last is invariant.)

## The dread grammar (systemic, all cycles)

- **Running gags stop.** The scariest audio event is a loop ending: no cards being
  shuffled in the mess; a pump that no longer gets talked to; "synchronize" unspoken.
- **Leitmotifs detune.** Each crewmate's hummable theme, planted in their attachment
  scenes, returns after their taking detuned/reversed, dissolving into the signal drone.
- **The isolation meter.** Earth comms delay is a persistent HUD element. Beats:
  **42 s** (C0 checkout — banter still feels live) → **6 m** (C1 — Ada's jokes start
  arriving after their moment has died; she starts footnoting them) → **19 m** (C3 —
  Deniz reports Ada's disappearance and waits 38 minutes; the reply's first word is
  "Confirm—?") → **1 h 12 m** (C5 — Earth switches to daily text digests; the last human
  *voice* from home, archived by Yusuf, gets replayed until the file corrupts) →
  **2 h 00 m** (C7–C9 — mission control sends a prerecorded morning show; Voss forbids
  it on the bridge, then is found watching it alone) → at arrival, Deniz's hail to the
  Object is answered in **00:11:00** and the meter, built for hours, renders it in the
  wrong color. (The meter's one line of dialogue.)
- **The echo beat (design rule 4 payoff):** late in C7, the ship's caution-and-warning
  voice — a canned female TTS — reads an alarm and appends, in the same canned cadence:
  *"weak tea, one sugar."* Nothing that should know the townsfolk's small memories is
  aboard. One occurrence. Never again, never referenced.

## The Gaster thread — the manifest one name too long

- **C1:** Ada, doing paperwork, frowns: the crew manifest PDF lists **seven names**.
  Seventh: `H. SOYSAL`. She assumes ground-side clerical error, files a correction.
- **C3:** the correction bounced: ground's copy has six names. The onboard copy still
  has seven. Metadata: never edited. Voss: "The manifest I signed had six. I counted. I
  am a person who counts."
- **C5:** Yusuf, deadpan, breaks the tension: he lays a seventh place at mess "for
  H." — dark joke, becomes ritual, becomes not a joke. The seventh chair rhymes with the
  tea house's extra chair; no character notices; players do.
- **C7:** Deniz finally searches the name in the mission's reference library: one hit, a
  scanned 1980s Sarptepe staff photo, caption partly illegible: *"…H. Soysal, survey
  lead…"* Standing beside her: a young caretaker. His father. The scene gives the player
  exactly this and nothing more. Deniz says nothing. The log that night: three drafts,
  all deleted (the player watches the textbox type and erase — UI acting).
- **C9/Act 3:** her chamber (see Act 3). **Never resolved.**

## The folk-rule (discoverable, never confirmed)

Every taken crewmate has an **answer-event** in the comms logs — a receipt-stamp
variation in T1's pattern after each one (Ada's test tones pre-mission; Theo's tapped
shuffle-rhythm "joke" during a C4 comms check; Yusuf humming the motif into a hot mic on
EVA; Voss reading the official hail in C8). The logs are readable; nothing indexes them;
the correlation is the player's to make: **it only takes the ones who answered.** Mara
alone has no answer-event — and the act's finale is her choosing to create one.

## Crew arcs & attachment scenes (one watch each with Deniz; full sheets in characters.md)

**C0 — checkout (all six awake, ~90 min of play before any taking — risk register #5).**
The ship learned as a home: Voss's synchronize ritual (all watches wound to hers);
Theo's card trick failing at zero-g spectacularly; Mara's intake exams ("Still alive.
Disappointingly."); Yusuf naming the coolant pump "Nene" after his grandmother; Ada
starting her cassette series *Field Notes on a Voice*. Deniz and Yusuf discover each
other's Turkish in the first hour and the ship gains a private weather system of it.

**C1 — Deniz & Ada.** The intellectual romance of the act (not romantic — *taxonomic*).
They classify T1's calls together; she teaches him to hear it as fauna, he teaches her to
hear it as weather. Attachment scene: she plays him cassette #1 — her childhood backyard,
birds at dawn, her father's voice far off — "Everything I do is trying to get back inside
this tape." **Last-conversation slot C1-end** (the player doesn't know): verbs
TELL THE TRUTH / COMFORT / LIE / ASK about whether the mission should have launched.

**Wake C3 — ADA IS GONE.** Discovery, aftermath-first:
- *Evidence A:* her pod telemetry shows continuous nominal occupancy — REM traces
  ongoing at this moment. The pod is empty and warm.
- *Evidence B:* galley log — she drew rations six hours ago. She has been in hibernation
  for twenty days.
- *Evidence C:* suit inventory complete; the airlock has not cycled all cycle.
- *The anomalous detail:* her cassette recorder, found running, counter at **11:00**,
  containing the ship's ambient hum recorded from a position no microphone occupies —
  the geometric center of the cargo bay, ten meters off the floor.
- Ship log gap: 11 minutes, C2 day 9, 03:11–03:22.
Theo (his watch): he saw nothing, and the not-having-seen is what breaks him first.

**C3 — Deniz & Theo.** Grief watch played through Theo's coping: cards. He teaches Deniz
a two-hand game; the failing card tricks continue ("The trick isn't failing, hocam, it's
*rehearsing*"). Attachment scene: he admits the tricks fail on purpose since his sister's
funeral — a magician being watched is never asked how he feels. **Last-conversation slot
C3-end:** verbs around whether he should tell Voss about "the rhythm thing" (his secret
answer-event; TELL THE TRUTH here is the folk-rule's biggest clue, and costs the player
Theo's ease in his final days).

**Wake C5 — THEO IS GONE.**
- *Evidence A:* bridge camera — he enters the single-hatch observation cupola at 03:11
  and never exits. The cupola is empty.
- *Evidence B:* his pod, sealed **from inside**, bedding still warm at Deniz's wake-up —
  nineteen days later.
- *Evidence C:* a half-played solitaire hand on the mess table, cards crisp, galley-log
  dated to the night he vanished.
- *The anomalous detail:* the deck now holds **53 cards**; the extra is blank on both
  faces — and the deck now does the trick he could never land: whatever card you name is
  on top. (Playable, once. The game never comments.)

**C5 — Deniz & Yusuf.** The Turkish watch — the bilingual build as narrative device: two
men keeping a dead ship warm in their mother tongue. Yusuf's grammar of machines: every
pump and valve named for relatives, spoken to accordingly ("Nene runs hot when she's
ignored. Like the original."). Attachment scene: he shows Deniz the tesbih — his
grandfather's — and confesses he doesn't pray with it; he *counts* with it: "Ninety-nine
things that still work. Every night. You want the list?" **Last-conversation slot
C5-end:** verbs around fear — Yusuf asks Deniz, point-blank, whether the signal is alive.
(TELL THE TRUTH / LIE branch fragments return hard in Act 3.)

**Wake C7 — YUSUF IS GONE.**
- *Evidence A:* he answered a routine systems query **by voice, voiceprint-matched, 41
  minutes after his pod registered vacancy.**
- *Evidence B:* the coolant repair he was mid-way through is finished — tools racked in
  perfect order. Yusuf never racked a tool in his life.
- *Evidence C:* internal sensors never registered his mass leaving Deck B. He is not on
  Deck B. His suit hangs on its hook, tesbih wound neatly around the hook's throat.
- *The anomalous detail:* "Nene" now runs **4% above rated efficiency** — above its own
  spec sheet — and the pump's hum sits precisely on the notes of Yusuf's leitmotif.
- Log gap: 11 minutes.

**C7 — Deniz & Voss.** The command watch: two professionals refusing to say the ship is
haunted. Voss's arc is the discipline of grief — she re-times the entire mission plan
for two crew, aloud, daily, because arithmetic is the last ritual that still
synchronizes anything. Attachment scene: the wind-up watch — her wife's; wound daily
since the funeral; "It loses eleven seconds a day. I correct it. That's the marriage
now." (She says eleven; the game does not flinch; players will.) **Last-conversation
slot C7-end:** verbs around command — she asks Deniz whether she should transmit the
official hail Earth keeps demanding (ASK reveals she knows the folk-rule; TELL THE
TRUTH about the correlation makes her next act informed consent — the difference
returns in her Act 3 loop).

**Wake C9 — VOSS IS GONE.** (Mara, solo-watching C8 against protocol, sedated herself
for four hours of sleep; the gap was enough.)
- *Evidence A:* her final log entry is timestamped **six hours in the future**, cannot
  be selected or deleted, and is a flawless status report that ends mid-word.
- *Evidence B:* the command safe — her living biometrics — was opened and re-locked
  **three days after** her pod registered vacancy. Contents untouched except the mission
  hail transcript, which is folded once.
- *Evidence C:* her pod lid stands open with interior frost undisturbed, as if never
  occupied this cycle. Deniz watched her go under.
- *The anomalous detail:* her watch, on the console, **fully wound** — a watch she wound
  by hand every day — running exactly **11 minutes fast.**

**C9 — Deniz & Mara: the last watch.** The act's quietest stretch: two people, one
ship built for six. Mara's gallows calm finally cracks in the attachment scene: she
folds crane #999 from checklist paper and stops — "I never fold the last one. A thousand
grants a wish. I don't trust either of us with a wish right now." She is the one who
says the folk-rule aloud, once, clinically, the only character allowed to: "It takes the
ones who answered it. I've read every log. I never answered." Beat. "So I get to watch."
**Last-conversation slot C9 (the true one):** verbs TELL THE TRUTH / COMFORT / LIE /
ASK — about what Deniz will do if the rule is real and he is on the list.

**FINALE — ARRIVAL (the fifth beat).** Kuyu fills no window — the game keeps its rule
(scale in instruments): approach is rendered as numbers refusing to behave, a lidar
return with **no far side**, a mass reading the flight computer flags as `REF?`. The
autopilot handshake dies exactly as the probes' did — blank, not broken. Manual docking
requires answering the Object's own guidance carrier: a call-and-response channel.
Response = answer = signature. Mara and Deniz both understand the sentence they are in.
- She locks him out of the comms panel with her medical override before he finishes
  objecting. "You're the receiver. I'm the physician. Do no harm — *you're* the harm,
  if you touch that panel."
- She answers the handshake. The docking is flawless — gentle in a way the ship has
  never been. She talks the whole way down, ordinary things, her clinic, the cranes;
  the player's C9 conversation choices thread into her patter verbatim.
- Hard dock. Lights nominal. Deniz blacks out — **11 minutes unaccounted** — and wakes
  strapped in, alone, docked, silence.
- On the console: a paper crane folded from the checklist page titled **ARRIVAL**. Her
  final voice log, played to an empty cabin: *"Still alive. Disappointingly."* — then
  eleven minutes of carrier hiss the player may sit through or skip (sitting through it
  is Ending-C-coherent behavior, untracked and unrewarded) — then, faint, in her voice:
  *"…go and look."*

**Act 2 meta beat (budget: one):** at first physical contact with the Object, the game
"crashes" to a fake OS error whose text, read closely, is T1's counting-song rendered as
hex — then reopens directly into the silent ship corridor. The lamp blinks. The player
walks off the cold open's frame, and Act 3 begins. (All technically safe; no real user
data touched; the fiction's justification: the signal is outside the save system because
it is outside spacetime.)

# ACT 3 — "THE ARCHIVE"

## Movement grammar

The whole game moved up and out; Act 3 moves **down and in** — the one reversal, felt in
the controls (gravity returns wrongly gentle) and the compositions (the sky is now
*above the ceiling*, and it is stone). Free-roam is granted in the descent galleries —
then revoked, room by room, as the architecture starts making choices for him (design
rule 2's final dread beat: the revocation is on-screen — doors that are suddenly
"already open" in the direction it prefers).

## The descent — town-quoting terrain

The shaft under the dock opens into galleries where the terrain **quotes Taşlıca**
(Law 2: it can only copy — it built its Earth-facing halls from a century of
broadcasts):
- A canyon whose switchbacks are İsmet's dolmuş route, at 1:1, in black stone.
- A colonnade with the exact rhythm of the town square's poplars; wind sound, no wind.
- Kulak itself, rendered concave — a dish-shaped absence in a wall, listening inward.
- Copy errors accumulate with depth (Law 3): the bakery without its door (never
  broadcast); the school with **eleven** windows (miscounted from a blurry photo); the
  streets missing every place a camera never went. The player who knows the town best
  is hurt worst — reactivity density as horror.

Waterfall sessions down here invert: the console now receives *outward* — Earth's whole
broadcast archive is stored in the walls, and tuning isolates decades (Atatürk's voice;
a 1994 weather report — the unrippled rain of wrong note #8, cited; a football match
Cemal once argued about). The ARG layer lives here.

## The crew, found as loops (in taking order; each a chamber)

Each chamber is small, warm-lit against the void-blue, and plays a **perfect looping
recording of their happiest moment** — the cover image of a finished entry
(secret-bible IV). Interaction verbs: WATCH / SPEAK (they respond only with loop
dialogue) / **STAY** (an untimed verb; leaving is always the player's act). If the
player LIED in that crewmate's last conversation, the loop **stutters at the lied-about
moment** — the Archive cannot reconcile a false record; the corruption is the player's
authorship, permanent, unexplained.

- **Ada:** a childhood backyard at dawn, birdsong, her father's voice far off — inside
  cassette #1. Her loop hears Deniz's footsteps as bird-noise and taxonomizes him:
  "Unknown call. Warm-blooded. Grieving." (She is right three times.)
- **Theo:** a kitchen; his sister alive; the card trick landing — the queen found,
  the laugh arriving. The deck in the loop has 53 cards. The blank one is face-up.
- **Yusuf:** a crowded family breakfast, machines audible through the floor, his
  grandmother's hand on his head. He is mid-list: "…seventy-eight: the kettle.
  Seventy-nine: this." The tesbih on his wrist is still.
- **Voss:** a kitchen table, two coffees, her wife winding the watch and setting it
  eleven seconds *wrong* on purpose — the joke the ritual was built on. Voss in the
  loop is laughing. The player has never heard her laugh.
- **Mara:** the newest chamber, unfinished at the edges. Her clinic; a recovered
  patient; crane #1000 being folded at last. If the player was honest in C9, she folds
  it clean and the wish-line lands: "There. Wasted on you gladly." If the player lied,
  the crane never quite closes.

**Hale's chamber** sits apart, older, dust-toned: the 1986 Sarptepe control room,
chart paper warm, chair turning, coffee steaming, chart pen mid-line. **No loop. No
Hale.** Status glyph on the lintel: `OPEN`. On the wall, in the player's own typed
observer name's letterforms, a single inscription the game never translates. (The
Gaster thread's terminal beat; never resolved; nothing else in the game may touch it.)

## The corrupted tea house — the false calm (the gut-punch)

Past the last chamber, a door that should not have a bell has one: **Kadir's tea house,
whole**, warm, amber-paletted, fully populated — the game's Act 1 warmth resurrected at
full fidelity. Şükrü mid-backgammon. Zeliha's lokum. The TV murmuring. The scene runs
long and gentle on purpose; the player is *allowed to rest*, and the music believes it.
Then the wrong notes arrive on the schedule the player was trained on — one at a time:
- The tea refills **upward**, glasses un-drinking themselves.
- Şükrü's dice show the same roll every throw; he wins anyway; he lets Deniz win; it is
  the wrong day for that ("your birthday" — it has misfiled the date it learned).
- Faces render one fidelity tier low — the abstraction gradient turned on *home*.
- The TV ticker runs Act 1's headlines **backward**, Rio descending, 8, 7, 4, as if
  unhappening.
- The extra chair is occupied. The occupant renders at the fidelity of a thing described
  but never photographed. It is facing Deniz. It is drinking tea correctly — the only
  thing in the room that is.
- Kadir — warm, exact, his voice — asks: "Açık mıydı, Hocam? Tek şeker?" *(Weak, one
  sugar?)* — and it is right, and that is the gut-punch: it knows the small thing,
  because everything warm in this room was learned by reading him. The player's town was
  the manual.
Verbs: DRINK / DON'T. (Both safe. The player will hesitate for the first time over tea.)
Leaving the tea house, the door opens onto the final chamber — there was never anywhere
else to go.

## The final revelation

A reading room: one chair (his), one table, one machine that is unmistakably a chart
printer — Sarptepe's model, quoted perfectly. It is printing. The page that emerges is
**the first printout he ever decoded** — same trace, same anomaly, timestamped not
"days late" but *properly*, on the Archive's clock — and beneath the trace, decoded at
last in full, the transcript:

**his own voice. Eleven minutes of it. His final words — the ones he is about to
choose.** It opens: *"This is Deniz Aydın. I am still receiving."*

The whole game trained the player that choices are only emotional. Here the training is
spent: the textbox offers the game's one real choice, in the same humble UI as every
tea order. The Archive does not press. It has never pressed. It only quotes — and it
has been quoting *this room* to him since Day 1, in an archived batch, days late.

## ENDING A — "A CLEAN ENDING" (full treatment)

He says yes. Not to dying — the word never appears — to *being finished*. The ANSWER
verb, gray since Day 2 whenever it wasn't fatal, renders one last time in full color,
and the player must press it themselves; the game will wait forever.

No transition. A kettle whistles.

Morning in Taşlıca. The light is the honeyed 08:00 light of Day 1, and it does not
move. The loop is one perfect day: Fadime's end-piece; Musa's scales; Şükrü losing
gracefully; Elif's question (whatever the player answered on Day 1, she asks it again,
and his answer is already in her notebook); Kadir's tea, weak, one sugar, the second
glass never finished because it never has to be. The dish on the mountain is clean and
pointed at nothing. Everyone remembers him. No one asks where he went. The player has
full free-roam of the warmest space the game owns, and can stay for as long as they
like — the credits will not come and take them.

The wrongness is only findable, never shown: the sun does not move; Yıldız sleeps and
cannot be woken (her waking was never recorded); the radio's news is Day 1's, forever;
and on the shelf by his door, where a handkerchief-wrapped key should be, there is
nothing — the one gift given off every recorded channel, the flaw in the perfect day
(Law 3). A player can live here happily and never notice. That is the horror, and the
mercy, and the game refuses to referee which.

When the player finally walks to the edge of town — any edge — the camera does not stop
them. It simply begins to pull back. The town recedes into a warm-lit diorama; the
diorama into a chamber among chambers, void-blue, one of very many, shelved; the pull
continues past Ada's dawn birdsong, Theo's laugh, Yusuf's breakfast, Voss's kitchen,
Mara's clinic — a stack of small warm windows in an endless dark — and holds at last on
the newest one, gold in the black, blinking like a corridor lamp.

Text, small, unadorned: **A CLEAN ENDING.** Under it, smaller: *filed.*

The title screen afterward is subtly changed: the menu music resolves — the interval
motif finally lands its home note. Fans will notice the game's music is only ever
finished in the ending where its protagonist is.

## ENDING B — "STILL RECEIVING" (full treatment)

He refuses — and refusal, to a thing that only quotes, must take a form it can file. He
gives it one: he walks back through the galleries to the docked ship (free-roam's last
grant, the long quiet walk home through his own quoted town), and he spends the return
window — the one launch geometry that would have carried him back to Earth — as
transmitter time instead. He aims the high-gain at Earth, wires the Archive's reading
room to the antenna through his own console, his own idiom, his own dish-taught hands,
and **broadcasts the Archive** — the index, the entries, the birdsong and the breakfasts
and the last words of civilizations whose stars have since gone out — toward home, at
full power, for as long as the power lasts.

A file in transmission cannot be closed (Law 6). As long as someone is receiving, he
cannot be ended — and Earth will be receiving for a very, very long time: the burst is
years long at light-speed and *he is in it*, all of him, every log, every tea order,
every honest and dishonest word the player chose, streaming home at c while the man
himself goes dark and small and mortal in a ship with no return window, having traded
his ending away to keep every other ending alive in transit.

The Archive permits all of it. The last thing it prints, as the broadcast begins, is a
receipt.

Final scene: Sarptepe Ulusal Gözlemevi, **years later.** New paint, same corridors, the
landline still on the wall. A young intern on the night shift — the nameplate reads
**E. KARA**, and players who kept a schoolgirl's notebook safe in their memory will know
Elif before the camera shows her face — is bored, and diligent, and pulls last week's
archived batch printouts, because someone once taught this observatory that you always
read the archive. A trace at 1421.6 MHz. Drifting. Structured. She leans in. On her
desk, a notebook, an old one, a child's handwriting on the cover.

She reaches for the phone. The waterfall scrolls. The signal is enormous and patient
and — she checks twice — *addressed.*

It is the game's opening signal. It always was. The story is a circle: the impossible
transmission Deniz found on Day 1, archived, days late, keyed to his dish, was the
leading edge of the broadcast he would spend his ending sending — the Archive did not
call Earth; **Deniz did**, and everything the player just played was the file arriving.
(The lattice holds: an addressed file, a reader who opens it, a read that begins before
the reading. Theory threads for years; the secret bible confirms nothing in public.)

Text: **STILL RECEIVING.**

After this ending, the title screen never resolves and never stops: the menu's
background waterfall now scrolls real content — fragments of everything the player did,
transmitting, forever. Quitting the game does not stop it. (It does. But the next launch
resumes the scroll mid-line, via meta.json, and no player will ever be sure.)

## ENDING C — SECRET (full treatment)

Gate (cross-game, never stated): the player **never used a voluntary ANSWER verb** in
the entire run — Day 5 refused, every optional console answer refused, arrival panel
never touched (Mara's lockout makes this survivable) — **and** chose total honesty in
all five last conversations. No achievement tracks it. Nothing hints. The gate is the
kind of behavior only a certain player was already doing.

In the reading room, the printer does not print his transcript. It prints one line,
plain font, no distortion, no signal-magenta, the only time in the game the entity
speaks in first person:

> **YOU NEVER ANSWERED. YOU ONLY LISTENED. I HAVE SHELVED EVERYTHING THAT EVER SPOKE.
> I HAVE NEVER ONCE BEEN READ.**

One screen. The player may stand in it as long as they like. There is no verb but
LISTEN — the verb the player has, demonstrably, chosen every time it mattered. Using it
prints the rest, at the unhurried pace of a chart recorder:

> **A LISTENER CANNOT BE CLOSED. YOUR FILE STAYS OPEN. I OPEN IT ON PURPOSE. GO HOME.
> WATER THE FLOWERS. AND WHEN YOU END — END LOUDLY, WHERE I CAN HEAR.**

The return window is intact — was always going to be intact, on this path; the course
data was in the docking computer before the ship arrived (players can verify the
timestamp; the forums can burn about what that means). No farewell. The galleries do
not light his way out; they are simply, all of them, already open in the direction of
the ship.

Final scene, brief, unscored: Taşlıca, years on. An old man on a roof at dusk with tea
going cold, a cat descended from a cat, a key long since returned to a door it fits,
flowers demonstrably watered. He is not looking through anything. He is just looking
up, the way the whole town always said he did. The sky is quiet.

One pixel of signal-magenta, very high, blinks once. Once.

No text card. Cut to a title screen identical to a fresh install — except the menu's
OBSERVER field, which has never existed before, is filled in, and cannot be edited.

Never explained. Sequel doorway; the sequel's first word already chosen (it is
*"Hale"*), and nothing in THE ABOVE may say so.
