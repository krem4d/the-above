# THE ABOVE — CHARACTERS

> Interlocks with `story/bible.md` and `story/secret-bible.md`. Design rule 4:
> reactivity density over world size — every named character remembers **one small
> thing** about the player, and late in Act 2 something that should not know these
> things echoes one back (canonical echo: Kadir's tea order, spoken by the ship's
> caution-and-warning voice, C7).

---

## DR. DENİZ AYDIN — protagonist

**Age 47. Radio astronomer, Sarptepe Ulusal Gözlemevi. The town calls him *Hoca*.**

**Biography.** Born in Taşlıca, 1979, son of İbrahim Aydın — the observatory's
caretaker for thirty years — and Nazmiye, who died when Deniz was small enough that his
memories of her are mostly other people's stories. Raised half in the town, half on the
mountain: he did his homework in the dish's control room, under charts annotated in a
handwriting he would spend his career unknowingly imitating (Hale Soysal's — he has
never once asked whose it was; the observatory taught him not to). At seven he watched
his father burn a box of printouts behind the equipment shed in November 1986 and
understood only that his father — a gentle, methodical man — was afraid of paper. He
became a radio astronomer the way some people become priests in answer to a doubt.
Studied in Ankara; postdoc abroad; came back. Everyone asked why. He says the seeing is
good at altitude. The truth is simpler and he has never said it: this is the only sky
he trusts, because it is the one his father cleaned the ear for.

**Shape of the man.** Never married; the town is his family and he is its one bachelor
elder, fed by rota. Keeps his father's maintenance logs in a drawer and reads them the
way other people read letters. Names things carefully and only once (the cat is Yıldız
— "star" — because a seven-year-old named her and the forty-seven-year-old respects the
decision). Kind in a *practical* register: fixes antennas, checks on roofs after storms,
answers every child's question as if it were peer review. His flaw, and the story's
lever: **he cannot leave a signal unread.** Curiosity, in him, is not enthusiasm — it is
compulsion wearing patience as a coat. The game's design rule ("fear is the price of
answers the player chooses to seek") is just Deniz, mechanized.

**Voice.** Dry, precise, self-deprecating in Turkish, slightly formal in English.
Understates on principle ("That's… nonstandard" is his scream). Slips into Turkish
under stress — with Yusuf, an entire private weather system of it. Never raises his
voice in the whole game; the script's loudest thing he does is stop moving.

**Player-facing verbs & mood range.** LISTEN / RECORD / LOG / (ANSWER); with people:
CHECK IN / DEFLECT / TELL THE TRUTH / ASK. Portrait moods: neutral, tired, wry,
listening (the signature — eyes off-frame, up), and one act-gated mood, *read*, used
only in Act 3.

**What he wants / what he needs / what he gets.** Wants: to be right about the signal.
Needs: to matter to someone the way the town matters to him (he never counts himself as
belonging to what he loves). Gets: the endings — finished (A), unfinishable (B), or the
only person the entity ever asked instead of read (C).

**Yıldız (the cat).** Tortoiseshell, elderly, opinionated. Carrier-detector (wrong
notes #5–6). In Ending A's loop she cannot wake; in Ending C the roof cat is her
descendant. She stays with Saniye when he leaves — the handoff is a send-off beat with
zero dialogue.

## THE TOWNSFOLK OF TAŞLICA (20)

Format — **Name — role.** The one small thing they remember about the player. *Send-off
line concept* (final EN/TR lines authored in locale files).

1. **Saniye Teyze — elderly neighbor, keeper of the key.** Remembers: he re-aims her TV
   antenna every autumn before the first storm, without being asked. *Send-off:* in her
   doorway, off every camera — the key wrapped in a handkerchief: don't leave the
   flowers without water; back before they notice. (The finale is built on this. See
   secret-bible Law 3.)
2. **Kadir — tea house owner.** Remembers: weak tea, one sugar; never finishes the
   second glass. *Send-off:* pours the second glass anyway and covers it with the
   saucer — "It'll wait."
3. **Zeliha — Kadir's wife, runs the kitchen.** Remembers: he pretends the lokum is too
   sweet and always takes a second piece. *Send-off:* a tin of lokum for the crew,
   labeled in her hand with six names — count them. (She wrote six. The game shows the
   label twice; in Act 2 the mess copy has a seventh line. Nobody wrote it.)
4. **Şükrü — retired teacher, backgammon partner.** Remembers: Deniz's birthday — lets
   him win once a year, exactly then. *Send-off:* concedes a game unasked: "Consider it
   your birthday." (Misfiled by the Archive in the corrupted tea house — it lets him win
   on the wrong day.)
5. **Fadime — baker.** Remembers: he likes the end-piece of the loaf; saves it daily.
   *Send-off:* the end-piece wrapped for a journey, "It keeps three days. Come back
   hungry."
6. **Musa — grocer (bakkal).** Remembers: buys cat food and red lentils, forgets bread
   (Fadime's covered). *Send-off:* "forgets" to charge him; slips cat food into the bag
   — "For the deputy." (Yıldız.)
7. **İsmet — dolmuş driver.** Remembers: night-shift men sit up front; holds the seat.
   *Send-off:* drives him to the airfield with the whole town's letters in a shoebox on
   the front seat — Deniz rides in the back for the first time; the empty front seat is
   the shot.
8. **Cemal — butcher, radio arguer.** Remembers: Deniz always backs whichever football
   team is losing. *Send-off:* "Up there, if there's a match — you know the rule.
   Losing side." (In Act 3's walls, a cited broadcast of the match they argued about.)
9. **Havva — postwoman.** Remembers: he reads addresses like coordinates, top-down.
   *Send-off:* hands him a blank, stamped envelope: "Address it from up there. I
   deliver everything." (Ending B is, structurally, him using it.)
10. **Hüsnü — barber.** Remembers: he falls asleep in the chair within two minutes,
    every time. *Send-off:* a precise, silent, final haircut; at the end he turns the
    chair *away* from the mirror. (He knows what his mirror has been doing. Wrong note
    #7 is his.)
11. **Nurten — pharmacist.** Remembers: his eye-drop brand, for screen strain; stocks
    it for him alone. *Send-off:* presses a full bottle into his hand: "There are no
    pharmacies where you're going. There is definitely screen strain."
12. **Elif — schoolgirl, 11, keeper of the notebook.** Remembers: every answer he has
    ever given her, verbatim, written down. *Send-off:* tears out the first page — his
    Day-1 answer in her handwriting — "So you don't contradict yourself up there."
    (Grows into **E. KARA**, the Ending-B intern. The notebook is on her desk, years
    later, next to the phone.)
13. **Tarık — muhtar (village head).** Remembers: Deniz's father — "İbrahim kept that
    ear clean thirty years." *Send-off:* the town's formal goodbye, two sentences,
    ending: "Your father listened downward, to us. Someone had to do the other
    direction eventually."
14. **Veli — shepherd.** Remembers: Deniz once spent a whole night in the pasture
    naming stars for the dogs. *Send-off:* "The flock faces the mountain some nights
    now. I don't correct them." (First finder of wrong note #2; his dogs are wrong note
    #9.)
15. **Osman — electrician.** Remembers: they grounded the dish together in the '09
    storm; Deniz held the ladder and recited resistor codes to keep him calm.
    *Send-off:* a coil of copper wire, "For luck. Ground yourself to something."
16. **Ayşe — nurse at the clinic.** Remembers: the tetanus shot he dreaded like a
    schoolboy in '17. *Send-off:* "They'll have doctors up there. Be exactly this much
    braver." (Rhymes with Mara — the game gives him a physician anyway.)
17. **Ferit — lycée physics teacher, friendly rival.** Remembers: lost a bet to Deniz
    about Mars's opposition date; paid in tea for a year. *Send-off:* "Double or
    nothing: come back and tell me what it was. *In person.* Those are the terms."
    (Ending C is the only ending that pays Ferit's bet.)
18. **Metin — observatory night operator, 58.** Remembers: Deniz hums when the data is
    good (learned it from him — Metin hummed first, decades ago). *Send-off:* not a
    line — he tips Kulak two degrees as the dolmuş passes below: a 32-meter wave.
    (Metin remembers Hale. He stops humming near T1 material from Day 2 and the game
    never says why. His only T1 line is the whiteboard doorway line, Day 6.)
19. **Ebru — PhD student, 27.** Remembers: Deniz mislabels Thursday printouts, every
    Thursday; she quietly corrects them. *Send-off:* named interim lead at Sarptepe;
    her goodbye is a clipboard: "Your Thursday labels are safe. Go." (Her 4 a.m.
    whiteboard dance is the act's one moment of pure joy; protect it in edit.)
20. **Doç. Dr. Orhan Tezcan — observatory director.** Remembers: talked Deniz out of
    quitting science in '13, over this same desk, with grant paperwork as a prop.
    *Send-off:* the apology of a careful man: "I said don't hang a career on a broken
    feed horn. I watched better men do it." Beat. "I never once watched one be right."
    (The 1986 file drawer is behind him in every office shot. He has never opened it.
    He knows exactly what is in it. These two facts coexist; that is his whole
    character.)

## THE CREW (5) — full sheets

Shared design contract: readable at 480p by **silhouette + signature prop + color**;
one running gag (whose *stopping* is the dread instrument); a hummable leitmotif that
detunes into the signal drone when taken; a disappearance built as 2–3 mutually
incompatible evidence pieces + exactly one anomalous detail (bible Act 2); and one
**canonical corrupted variant** (the "what the signal did to them" design — fanart/AU
prompt; appears in-game only as their Act 3 loop's corruption when lied to).

---

### CMDR. INGRID VOSS — commander
- **Silhouette:** tallest crew member; straight-backed; hair in a flat, severe knot —
  reads as a vertical line with squared shoulders.
- **Prop:** a mechanical wind-up wristwatch (her late wife's; loses 11 s/day; corrected
  daily — "That's the marriage now").
- **Color:** steel gray. **Leitmotif concept:** a metronome-strict clock figure in
  even eighths, always ending one note short of resolution; instrument voice: dry
  woodblock + celesta.
- **Running gag:** the "synchronize" ritual — every watch handover, all watches wound
  and set to hers; she says "synchronize" like a benediction. *Gag-stop:* after C8 no
  one says it; in C9 Deniz says it alone to an empty bridge, once, and the game does
  not score it.
- **Arc:** discipline as love-language; command as grief management; her answer-event
  is *duty itself* — she reads the official hail she refused to delegate, in full
  knowledge (informed consent if the player told her the truth in C7).
- **Disappearance (wake C9):** future-timestamped log ending mid-word / command safe
  opened by her living biometrics three days after her pod emptied / pod frost
  undisturbed though Deniz watched her go under. **Anomalous detail:** the watch on the
  console — fully wound, exactly 11 minutes fast.
- **Loop (Act 3):** her wife setting the watch 11 seconds wrong on purpose; Voss
  laughing — a sound the player has never heard.
- **Canonical corrupted variant:** the *Unwound* — silhouette unchanged but the knot
  of hair unbound and drifting as if underwater; the watch face on her wrist grown to
  cover the forearm, hands sweeping backward, no numerals; her even-eighths motif
  played rubato, which on her is obscene.

### THEO OKAFOR — pilot
- **Silhouette:** compact, rounded shoulders, perpetual lean — a man mid–card-flourish
  even standing still; flight jacket half-zipped.
- **Prop:** a worn card deck (52; later 53).
- **Color:** warm orange. **Leitmotif concept:** a sly two-bar swing lick that always
  fluffs its final note — deliberately; muted trumpet feel in chip-voice.
- **Running gag:** card tricks that always fail ("The trick isn't failing, it's
  *rehearsing*"). Truth beneath: they fail on purpose, since his sister's funeral — a
  magician being watched is never asked how he feels. *Gag-stop:* the mess with no
  shuffle-sound is the C5 wake-up's first wrongness, before any evidence.
- **Arc:** charm as armor; his answer-event is a joke (tapped shuffle-rhythm into a
  live comms check — it tapped back; he told no one; TELL THE TRUTH in his last
  conversation makes him confess it to Voss and spend his last days lighter or
  heavier depending on her reply).
- **Disappearance (wake C5):** enters single-hatch cupola on camera, never exits, cupola
  empty / pod sealed from inside, bedding warm 19 days on / half-played solitaire hand
  dated to the vanishing night. **Anomalous detail:** the deck holds 53 cards, one
  blank both faces — and now any card you name is on top. Playable once.
- **Loop (Act 3):** his sister's kitchen; the queen found; the laugh arriving. Blank
  card face-up in the loop.
- **Canonical corrupted variant:** the *Dealt* — silhouette fanned at the edges like a
  spread hand; face rendered as the blank card (featureless, bordered); his fluffed
  final note now lands perfectly every repeat, which is how you know it isn't him.

### DR. MARA ITO — flight surgeon
- **Silhouette:** small, still, precise; sleeves always exactly folded; reads as a
  compact triangle beside Voss's line.
- **Prop:** paper cranes folded from checklist pages (numbered; stops at #999).
- **Color:** pale green. **Leitmotif concept:** a lullaby fragment in 5/4 — a meter
  that never quite rocks — music-box voice, always pianissimo.
- **Running gag:** morning vitals delivered with the same words: "Still alive.
  Disappointingly." *Gag-stop / inversion:* her final voice log at arrival is the gag,
  played straight to an empty cabin, then eleven minutes of hiss, then "…go and look."
- **Arc:** the one who never answered — the folk-rule's control group and its proof.
  Gallows humor as bedside manner; the only character allowed to say the rule aloud.
  Her ending is the game's definition of choice: she knew, and answered anyway, for
  someone else.
- **Disappearance (arrival):** she locks Deniz out with her medical override, answers
  the docking handshake, talks him down through a flawless dock threading his C9
  conversation back to him verbatim; his 11-minute blackout; the cabin empty.
  **Anomalous detail:** a crane on the console folded from the checklist page titled
  ARRIVAL — crane **#1000**, which she swore never to fold.
- **Loop (Act 3):** her clinic, a recovered patient, #1000 folded clean — "There.
  Wasted on you gladly." (Lied-to variant: the crane never quite closes.)
- **Canonical corrupted variant:** the *Thousandth* — silhouette identical but rendered
  in folded planes, paper-creased; the crane flock orbits her at reading-lamp height;
  her 5/4 lullaby finally in 4/4, rocking properly, wrong.

### YUSUF DEMİR — engineer
- **Silhouette:** broad, low center of gravity, tool harness adding a stepped outline;
  the only crew silhouette that reads *heavier* than the ship around him.
- **Prop:** his grandfather's tesbih (he doesn't pray with it; he counts with it —
  "Ninety-nine things that still work. Every night.").
- **Color:** copper. **Leitmotif concept:** a warm makam-inflected phrase over a
  machine-room ostinato — the one theme with a drone *built in*, so its dissolution
  into the signal is the subtlest and worst.
- **Running gag:** names every machine after his relatives and speaks to them
  accordingly ("Nene runs hot when she's ignored. Like the original."). *Gag-stop:*
  after C6, machines get serviced in silence; the ship stops being a family and starts
  being equipment — the crew's grief made audible as *professionalism*.
- **Arc:** the bilingual heart (Deniz's private weather system of Turkish); animist
  tenderness as engineering method; his answer-event is affection itself — humming the
  signal's motif to the antenna he was healing, because he has never once worked on a
  machine without talking to it.
- **Disappearance (wake C7):** voiceprint-matched answer to a systems query 41 minutes
  after pod vacancy / his repair finished and tools racked in an order he never used in
  his life / sensors never logged his mass leaving Deck B, where he is not; suit on its
  hook, tesbih wound around the hook's throat. **Anomalous detail:** "Nene" runs 4%
  above her own spec sheet, humming his leitmotif on pitch.
- **Loop (Act 3):** the crowded family breakfast, machines audible through the floor,
  his grandmother's hand on his head, the count at "…seventy-nine: this."
- **Canonical corrupted variant:** the *Ninety-Ninth* — silhouette hung with beads at
  machine scale, each bead a tiny warm-lit window (chambers — the fanart engine);
  tesbih circling without a hand; his drone present, his melody absent.

### DR. ADA REYES — SETI liaison
- **Silhouette:** angular, quick, scarf-tail always in motion — the one crew silhouette
  with a moving part; headphones around neck as a fixed collar shape.
- **Prop:** a handheld cassette recorder (series title: *Field Notes on a Voice*).
- **Color:** violet. **Leitmotif concept:** a rising three-note "contact call" answered
  by its own inversion — call-and-response built into the theme; the crew's only motif
  in a major key, which is the trap.
- **Running gag:** taxonomizes everything like birdsong, humans included ("Unknown
  call. Warm-blooded. Overcaffeinated."). *Gag-stop:* C3's wake-up — a ship without her
  narration is the first time the player hears how loud the ventilation is.
- **Arc:** rigor as wonder; the thesis-line carrier ("Every protocol we have assumed
  the message would be old, distant, and indifferent. This one is none of those.");
  first taken *because* first to answer — eagerness as signature. Her Act 1
  introduction makes her the only crew member the town has fed, which is why her loss
  lands in Taşlıca-register, not ship-register.
- **Disappearance (wake C3):** pod telemetry mid-REM at discovery, pod empty and warm /
  galley draw six hours ago against twenty days asleep / suits complete, airlock never
  cycled. **Anomalous detail:** her recorder running, counter at 11:00, holding the
  ship's hum recorded from a point in mid-air no microphone occupies.
- **Loop (Act 3):** the childhood backyard at dawn inside cassette #1; taxonomizes
  Deniz's footsteps: "Unknown call. Warm-blooded. Grieving."
- **Canonical corrupted variant:** the *Field Note* — silhouette intact but rendered in
  spectrogram hatching, scarf-tail become a waterfall trace; where her face was, the
  interval motif *as image*; her major-key call still rising, the response now arriving
  **before** it.

### The sixth sheet (unwritten by design)

`H. SOYSAL` has no character sheet. She has a dossier (secret-bible §V), a chamber, a
handwriting, and a hook on a corridor wall. If any future document gives her a
silhouette, a color, or a line of dialogue, that document is wrong. She is the shape
the game keeps by *not* drawing it.
