# THE ABOVE — CLIP ANCHORS (7), STORYBOARDED

> Plan §4: each anchor is a 15–60 s moment engineered to survive a stream clip and a
> 9:16 crop, with a hook inside the first 2 seconds. These are *design constraints on
> the scenes themselves* — the scene is built so the clip falls out of it; nothing is
> staged for camera that the player doesn't also experience.
>
> Global 9:16 rules: 640×360 crops to a 202×360 column — every anchor's critical action
> is composed inside a center-safe column (dialogue box spans full width but its
> *speaker name + first clause* must land center); signal-magenta is the thumbnail
> flare (any frame containing it is legibly ominous at 120 px tall); UI text ≥16 px so
> it survives platform re-compression at 4× nearest-neighbor export (built-in share
> tool is the canonical capture path).

## ① THE ARCHIVED PRINTOUT — "We heard it days after it spoke." *(Day 1, S5)*

- **Hook in 2 s:** extreme close crop of a chart printer head hammering out a spike —
  the trace physically taller than the paper's rule lines — over the dry rattle of a
  dot-matrix. Motion + sound + wrongness before any face.
- **Beat list (~35 s):**
  1. (0–2 s) Printer close-up; the anomalous spike prints.
  2. (2–8 s) Pull back: Deniz shelving boring archives, one page in his hand mid-turn;
     his idle animation stops. (The stop IS the acting.)
  3. (8–16 s) He lays the page on the light table; the waterfall UI mirrors it; the
     trace glows the game's first-ever pixels of signal-magenta.
  4. (16–26 s) He checks the date stamp; sprite looks at wall calendar; back at page.
     Textbox: "It arrived Tuesday." Beat. "It's Friday."
  5. (26–35 s) The line (below), then verbs RE-PRINT / COMPARE / SAY NOTHING fade in —
     ending the clip on a choice the viewer can't make. (Comment bait by design.)
- **The exact line:** **"We heard it days after it spoke."**
- **9:16 notes:** printer head, light table, and textbox name-column all sit in the
  center column; calendar and date stamp are staged center-frame (they're the plot).
  Thumbnail frame: beat 3, the magenta trace on the light table. The dot-matrix rattle
  is the clip's audio hook — it survives phone speakers.

## ② THE MICROWAVE — the peryton debunking montage *(Day 3, S3)*

- **Hook in 2 s:** a kitchen microwave on an evidence table in a radio observatory,
  lit like an interrogation. No context. (The absurd image is the hook; it is also
  100% real science history — the devlog thread writes itself.)
- **Beat list (~50 s, montage rhythm — the game's one comedic cut pattern):**
  1. (0–2 s) The microwave, spotlit. Caption card in-fiction: RFI SUSPECT 03.
  2. (2–10 s) Smash-cuts: three "detections" pinned to lunch hours; Ebru holding her
     soup cup like a suspect in a line-up; Metin unplugging the microwave with bomb-
     disposal gravity.
  3. (10–22 s) The re-test: door opened before the timer ends → broadband chirp
     splatters the waterfall. Ebru's portrait: horror. It was the soup all along.
  4. (22–34 s) Clean sweep montage: chirps GONE from new data; checklist items striking
     through; the room begins, cautiously, to laugh.
  5. (34–44 s) The laugh dies in one cut: the waterfall, quiet band — **T1 still
     there.** Patient. In beam 2. Magenta in a suddenly unfunny frame.
  6. (44–50 s) Deniz, to the unplugged microwave, deadpan (line below). Hold.
- **The exact line:** **"You're free to go."** *(secondary clip line, beat 5, Ebru:
  "It survived the soup.")*
- **9:16 notes:** microwave centered on its table; the checklist is a vertical prop
  (reads perfectly in a phone crop); beat 5's cut-to-waterfall must keep the T1 trace
  in the center column — the joke-to-dread reversal is the shareable unit, so beats
  4→5→6 must survive being clipped alone (they do: laugh, silence, punchline).

## ③ THE SECOND DISH — "We hear it too." *(Day 4, S3)*

- **Hook in 2 s:** a 1980s rotary landline ringing in a dark control room, its pull-cord
  shadow swinging; the ring is mixed louder than anything in the game so far.
- **Beat list (~40 s):**
  1. (0–2 s) The phone rings twice before Deniz can cross the room (sprite RUN — the
     first time the walk-speed rule breaks all game; viewers who've watched streams
     notice, which is the point).
  2. (2–10 s) Receiver up. Four seconds of transatlantic static — held, no subtitles.
     The game makes the viewer wait inside the hiss.
  3. (10–18 s) Textbox, plain: "Dr. Aydın." Beat. The line (below).
  4. (18–30 s) Reaction chain, no words: Deniz's hand finds the desk; Metin in the
     doorway; Ebru sits down on the floor, out of frame bottom — her disappearance
     downward is the laugh-sob of the clip.
  5. (30–40 s) The quiet second hook: Petrides continues — "It's only in one beam here
     as well. *Our* beam two. Doctor — whose instrument does that?" Cut to black on the
     question.
- **The exact line:** **"We hear it too."**
- **9:16 notes:** the phone lives center-frame from Day 1 set dressing onward (it's
  been planted in every observatory shot). The static beat needs a visible waveform
  flicker in the wall speaker prop so the wait reads visually with sound off —
  captions burn in automatically via the share tool.

## ④ THE WHITEBOARD — the eleven minutes *(Day 6, S2 + S4 — the keystone)*

- **Hook in 2 s:** a marker squeaking one number onto an empty board: **11 min.**
  Nothing else. (Numbers-as-dread is the game's thesis; this clip is its proof.)
- **Beat list (~60 s, two movements — the clip contains BOTH beats of the scene):**
  1. (0–2 s) "11 min" written; squeak mixed high.
  2. (2–14 s) RELIEF: d ≤ c·t/2 → 5.5 light-minutes → "≈ 99 million km" circled;
     Ebru's terrible 4 a.m. joy-dance (2 s max — protect it in edit).
  3. (14–20 s) Deniz: "That's inside the orbit of Mars. It's not light-years away.
     It's HERE." Lokum at 4 a.m. Warmth peaks.
  4. (20–26 s) Title card, in-fiction: THE SAME WHITEBOARD. — 11 HOURS LATER. The board
     now holds a second number: **29.8 AU = 4 h 08 m.** Both circled. Three people not
     talking.
  5. (26–40 s) The arithmetic of dread, drawn live: round trip 8 h 16 m vs. 11 min; a
     line under it; the departure-time subtraction written like a confession: the reply
     left 3 h 57 m *before* the question.
  6. (40–52 s) The line (below). No sting. No music. Nobody moves.
  7. (52–60 s) Metin, from the doorway, his only T1 line: "Erase one. Whichever lets
     you sleep." He goes. Neither number is erased. Hold on the two circles.
- **The exact line:** **"Either the physics is wrong, or it answered before it was
  asked."**
- **9:16 notes:** THE WHITEBOARD IS COMPOSED AS A 9:16 OBJECT — tall and narrow, both
  circled numbers stacked vertically in the center column (this is why the scene works
  cropped: the board was designed phone-first). Characters stand frame-left/right and
  are croppable losses. Thumbnail frame: beat 4, two circles, no people. This is the
  scene with the disproportionate polish budget (risk register #4); the clip is its
  acceptance test.

## ⑤ "IT KNOWS WHICH DISH IS YOURS" *(Day 7, S1 — the blind test)*

- **Hook in 2 s:** a split-screen the game has never used before: five waterfall
  panes, five station names, one sky. Four panes of noise; one pane of language,
  magenta, alive.
- **Beat list (~45 s):**
  1. (0–2 s) The five-pane split; the wrong pane glowing.
  2. (2–12 s) The schedule randomizes (envelope prop; Metin blind too); stations swap;
     the language *follows Kulak's pane*. Every pass.
  3. (12–22 s) The cruel controls, intercut: Deniz remote-driving Millbrook — noise;
     Ebru seated at Kulak — language. It is not the man. It is his dish.
  4. (22–30 s) Ada into her cassette recorder, flat: "Correlation persists. Test 31 of
     31." *Click.* (The click is the beat's percussion.)
  5. (30–38 s) Ada's thesis line to the room: "Every protocol we have assumed the
     message would be old, distant, and indifferent. This one is none of those."
  6. (38–45 s) Deniz at the window, Kulak beyond, the line (below), cut on the last
     word — no reaction shot. The clip ends before the room can.
- **The exact line:** **"It knows which dish is yours."**
- **9:16 notes:** the five panes stack vertically in portrait crop (the split-screen is
  authored twice: landscape row and portrait stack; the share tool exports the stack).
  Kulak-through-window in beat 6 is center-column. Thumbnail: beat 1's five-pane frame
  — four gray, one magenta — the game's brand grammar in a single image.

## ⑥ THE SEND-OFF & LAUNCH *(Day 7, S5–S6)*

- **Hook in 2 s:** an old woman's hands wrapping a house key in a handkerchief.
  No faces, no UI. (Warmth is this clip's hook; it is the algorithm's counter-programming
  against the other six.)
- **Beat list (~60 s):**
  1. (0–2 s) The key wrapped.
  2. (2–8 s) Saniye's doorway, no crowd, no phones: "Don't leave the flowers without
     water. You'll be back before they notice."
  3. (8–24 s) The town's goodbye chain, one line per face, fast but unhurried: Fadime's
     wrapped end-piece → Şükrü conceding the game ("Consider it your birthday") →
     Elif's torn notebook page → Kadir covering the second tea glass with the saucer:
     "It'll wait."
  4. (24–32 s) İsmet's dolmuş pulls away; the empty front seat; the shoebox of letters;
     Metin tips Kulak two degrees as the road passes below — the 32-meter wave.
  5. (32–44 s) Smash cut: gantry steam, three languages of checklist, the tea house TV
     crammed with the whole town — the extra chair empty at the back of frame.
  6. (44–58 s) Capsule interior; Ada across the aisle; engine roar swallows the mix —
     except, planted underneath it, the signal's interval motif. The music knew.
  7. (58–60 s) Cut to black on max thrust. Title: END OF ACT ONE.
- **The exact line:** **"Don't leave the flowers without water."**
- **9:16 notes:** hands, key, doorway, dolmuş seat, and the covered tea glass are all
  native close-ups (already portrait-shaped). The TV-crowd shot keeps the extra chair
  inside the center column — the one dread pixel in sixty warm seconds; eagle-eyed
  reposters are the distribution plan. Audio note: the motif-under-engines must survive
  phone-speaker compression — it is doubled an octave up at −18 dB in the share-tool
  mixdown.

## ⑦ THE CORRIDOR, WITH ONE DIFFERENCE *(post-credit)*

- **Hook in 2 s:** it's the cold open again — viewers of any stream have seen this
  exact frame; recognition IS the hook. Same corridor, same hum, same amber blink.
- **Beat list (~30 s):**
  1. (0–8 s) Frame-for-frame replay of the cold open. Identical durations. The
     confidence to be boring is the trick.
  2. (8–16 s) The lamp's blink cycle; on the sixth blink, one frame of magenta —
     exactly as before. (Freeze-framers get their reward: it matches. Trust the VOD.)
  3. (16–26 s) Nothing announces it. The wall simply has **seven suit hooks: five
     suits, two empty.** No zoom, no sting, no camera help of any kind.
  4. (26–30 s) Hold four seconds longer than the cold open held. Cut to title screen;
     the menu's version string has gained one character.
- **The exact line:** *(no dialogue — the clip's "line" is a shot)* — **the seventh
  hook.** Community caption bait; the game itself never names it.
- **9:16 notes:** the hook wall was composed inside the center column in BOTH versions
  precisely so side-by-side portrait comparisons work (old left, new right, hooks
  aligned to the same pixels). The share tool's A/B export (cold open vs. post-credit,
  synced) exists for this anchor alone. Thumbnail: the two walls side by side, no
  caption. Let the comments count.
